# Passport & Trust Boundaries

The **Decision Packet (Passport)** is the definitive artifact representing your professional identity within the CareerTwin Marketplace. It is generated by compiling your Candidate Profile, Evaluation Runs, and Evidence Engine data into a highly structured JSON object (`PassportSchema`).

## Core Concept: Local-First Privacy
Your data never leaves your machine unless explicitly published. The `.careertwin/` directory acts as your private vault. When you run `ct passport build`, the Candidate OS processes your private data and filters it through strict **Trust Boundaries**.

## The Three Trust Boundaries

When your Passport is synchronized with the live CareerTwin backend, the visibility of its fields is strictly governed by three tiers:

### 1. Private (Local Only)
Data that remains strictly within your `.careertwin/` filesystem and is **never** sent in the Passport payload.
- Personal phone numbers
- Exact home addresses
- Unfiltered, raw CV text
- Private tracking data for rejected/archived applications

### 2. Founder (Authorized Viewing)
Data that is included in the Passport but is strictly gated on the live CareerTwin backend. Only verified Founders who match your `startupFitIndicators` or whom you have explicitly approved can view these fields.
- Full Name
- Primary Email Address
- Detailed Experience Highlights
- Links to GitHub/LinkedIn
- Technical Gap Analysis (Evaluation outputs)

### 3. Public (Marketplace Search)
Data that is freely indexable by the CareerTwin search engine and visible to anyone browsing the marketplace.
- `roleTags` (e.g., "Full Stack Developer")
- `skillTags` (e.g., "React", "Node.js")
- `domainTags` (e.g., "E-Commerce", "SaaS")
- `founderIntro` (Your 2-sentence hook)
- Aggregated fit scores

---

## Marketplace Actions

### Publishing your Passport
```bash
ct passport publish
```
This command establishes an authenticated bridge with the Supabase backend. It reads your local `.careertwin/artifacts/passports/passport.json`, strips any data flagged as `Private`, and syncs it. Your profile becomes "Active" on the marketplace.

### Unpublishing your Passport
```bash
ct passport unpublish
```
If you accept an offer or simply want to hide your profile, this command instantly revokes marketplace visibility. Your data remains perfectly intact in your local OS, but Founders will no longer be able to discover or view your Passport online.
